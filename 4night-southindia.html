4 Night South India

(Madurai 2nt + Rameshwaram 2nt)

Arrival at Madurai and sightseein

Day 01:  You will be picked up from Madurai airport and transferred to your hotel. After checking in and freshening up, you can start your sightseeing tour of Madurai, one of the oldest cities in India and a major pilgrimage destination.

You can visit the famous Meenakshi Amman Temple, a magnificent example of Dravidian architecture and one of the most sacred temples for Hindus. You can admire the intricate carvings, colorful gopurams, and the hall of thousand pillars. You can also witness the evening aarti ceremony at the temple.

You can also visit other attractions in Madurai, such as Thirumalai Nayak Mahal, a 17th-century palace with a fusion of Islamic and Rajput styles; Gandhi Museum, which displays memorabilia related to Mahatma Gandhi and the Indian freedom struggle; Alagar Kovil, a Vishnu temple located on a hill; and Vandiyur Mariamman Teppakulam, a huge tank with an island temple in the middle. You can spend the night at your hotel in Madurai.

Madurai sightseeing’s

Day 02:  After breakfast, visit’s Thiruparankundram Murugan Temple, A temple dedicated to Lord Murugan. Later visit Koodal Azhagar Temple, An ancient temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu. In afternoon visit’s Vandiyur Mariamman Teppakulam, A temple tank located near to Meenakshi Temple and Yanaimalai, An elephant-shaped hill located near Madurai. Later in evening visit St. Mary’s Cathedral, A Roman Catholic cathedral in Madurai. You can spend the night at your hotel in Madurai.

Madurai to Rameshwaram (170 km / 3 hours)

Day 03:  After breakfast, you will check out from your hotel and drive to Rameshwaram, an island town connected to the mainland by a bridge.

Rameshwaram is one of the four char dhams or holy places for Hindus, as it is believed to be the place where Lord Rama built a bridge to Lanka to rescue his wife Sita from Ravana. On arrival, you will check in to your hotel and rest for a while.

In the afternoon, you can visit the Ramanathaswamy Temple, one of the 12 Jyotirlinga or shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its long corridors, ornate pillars, and 22 sacred wells. You can also take a dip in the sea and the wells as part of the ritual. Later you can spend the night at your hotel in Rameshwaram.

Rameshwaram sightseeing

Day 04: After breakfast, You can also visit other places of interest in Rameshwaram, such as Agnitheertham, a sacred bathing spot on the seashore; Dhanushkodi, a ghost town that was destroyed by a cyclone in 1964; Pamban Bridge, an engineering marvel that connects Rameshwaram to the mainland; and Abdul Kalam Memorial, a museum dedicated to the former president and scientist of India. You can spend the night at your hotel in Rameshwaram.

Rameshwaram – Madurai Airport Drop

Day 05:  After breakfast, you can check-out from your hotel and proceed to your onward destination. You can take back some wonderful memories of your Kerala trip with you.
